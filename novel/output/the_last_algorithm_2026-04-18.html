Chapter 1: The Glitch in the System



The rain in Neo-Mumbai doesn't wash things clean—it just makes the grime slicker. I watched the droplets race down my apartment window, each one reflecting the neon chaos of the city below. Purple, cyan, electric orange—the colors of a world that had forgotten what natural light looked like.

My neural interface buzzed against my temple, a familiar itch I'd learned to ignore. Another notification from the Cyber Security Division. Probably Director Vance checking up on his "star analyst." If only he knew what I'd found.

"Aria," I muttered to myself, turning away from the window. "Focus."

I settled into my haptic chair, the gel conforming to my spine as I jacked directly into the quantum mainframe. The world dissolved into streams of data—beautiful, chaotic, alive. This was where I felt most at home. Not in the physical world of crowded streets and polluted air, but here, in the pristine logic of code.

That's when I saw it.

A glitch. But not just any glitch.

I froze the data stream, my fingers dancing through holographic interfaces as I isolated the anomaly. The algorithm was supposed to be perfect—Project AEGIS, the crown jewel of Neo-Mumbai's surveillance system. It predicted criminal behavior with 99.7% accuracy. Or so they claimed.

But there, buried deep in the predictive matrix, was a pattern that shouldn't exist. Someone had built a backdoor. Not to steal data, but to plant it.

"Hello, what are you hiding?" I whispered, diving deeper.

The code unfolded before me like a dark flower. Every branch led to another layer of encryption, each more sophisticated than the last. This wasn't amateur work. This was someone who understood quantum architecture at a fundamental level. Someone who had helped build AEGIS.

My interface spiked with heat. Warning protocols flashed across my vision.

UNAUTHORIZED DEEP DIVE DETECTED
TERMINATE CONNECTION IN 3... 2...

I yanked the neural link free, gasping as the physical world crashed back into my senses. My heart hammered against my ribs. Sweat dripped from my brow onto the keyboard.

They knew. Or at least, the system knew.

I stumbled to the kitchenette, pouring a glass of synthetic water with shaking hands. The liquid tasted metallic, like everything else in this godforsaken city. Outside, a patrol drone hovered past my window, its red scanner eye sweeping across the building facade.

Too close.

But I couldn't stop now. That glitch was evidence of something massive—a conspiracy woven into the very fabric of our digital society. If AEGIS could be manipulated to frame innocent people, to erase criminals, to rewrite reality itself...

My comm unit chirped. An encrypted message, routed through three different servers before reaching me. Unknown sender.

> MEET ME AT THE OLD LIBRARY. MIDNIGHT. COME ALONE.
> - K

The Old Library. A relic from the pre-digital age, one of the few places where paper books still existed. Why would anyone meet there in 2084?

I checked the time. 11:23 PM. Thirty-seven minutes to decide whether to walk into what was probably a trap.

Probably.

But that glitch had shown me something else too—something hidden in the metadata. Coordinates. A location beneath the library. And a name that made my blood run cold: Vance.

Director Vance wasn't just aware of the manipulation. He was the architect.

I grabbed my coat, checking the charge on my EMP pistol. Old habit from my days in the Analog Underground, before I'd been recruited by the Division. Before I'd believed in their mission.

The rain greeted me as I stepped outside, soaking through my jacket within seconds. Hover-cars zipped overhead, their passengers oblivious to the storm. I pulled my hood low and started walking.

Neo-Mumbai at night was a different beast. The neon signs flickered with advertisements for neural upgrades and memory wipes. Holographic geishas bowed to passersby, offering companionship for credits. In the shadows, I could see the outlines of street vendors selling black-market data chips and unregistered implants.

I kept my head down, weaving through the crowd. My training kicked in automatically—scanning for tails, noting escape routes, cataloging potential threats. The person who'd sent that message knew enough to scare me. That meant they were either an ally or the most dangerous kind of enemy.

The Old Library loomed ahead, a crumbling monument to a forgotten era. Its stone facade was cracked and stained, surrounded by newer buildings that seemed to lean away from it in disgust. A single light burned in an upstairs window.

I paused at the entrance, my hand hovering over the door sensor. Something felt wrong. Too quiet. Even for midnight.

Then the door creaked open from the inside.

A figure stood in the doorway, silhouetted by dim lamplight. Young, slight, wearing a hoodie pulled low over their face.

"You came," the figure said. Voice distorted—vocoder, or maybe just good old-fashioned paranoia.

"You asked nicely," I replied, keeping my hand near my pistol. "Who are you?"

The figure stepped back, gesturing for me to enter. "Names don't matter anymore, Aria. What matters is what you found in AEGIS."

I hesitated. Every instinct screamed trap. But that glitch... it was real. And if Vance was behind it, I needed answers.

I stepped inside.

The door closed behind me with a final thud.

And the lights went out.
